Fluid Dynamics and CFD

  • Fluid dynamics governs interaction between geometry, forces, transport
  • Central in aerodynamics, propulsion, biomedical and environmental systems
  • CFD solves Navier–Stokes equations on discretized domains
  • Provides detailed velocity, pressure, turbulence fields
  • Enables study of complex, non-linear flows

Power vs Cost of CFD

  • CFD gives high-fidelity flow information
  • Works on complex geometries and conditions
  • Requires large computational resources
  • Simulations can take hours to days
  • Produces massive high-dimensional data which usually are limited to few cases

CFD as Data Problem

  • Each simulation → millions of spatial points
  • Multiple physical variables per point
  • Strong dependency on geometry
  • Few labelled samples → limited datasets
  • Leads to Large-p, Small-n regime

Machine Learning in CFD

  • ML extracts patterns from complex data
  • Used for:
    • flow reconstruction
    • simulation acceleration
    • turbulence modelling
  • Typically acts as surrogate or accelerator
  • Focus on reproducing known physical quantities

A Shift of Paradigm: From Simulation to Inference

  • Move from approximating CFD → learning from CFD
  • CFD seen as data generator
  • Flow fields encode geometry + physics + conditions
  • Use flows as informative signatures
  • Goal: infer hidden system properties

Core Challenges

  • Extremely high-dimensional inputs
  • Limited labelled simulations
  • Strong geometry-induced variability
  • Different domains → hard comparison
  • Learning becomes ill-posed

Limits of Standard Approaches

  • Dimensionality reduction learns global modes
  • Captures dominant energetic structures
  • Misses localized, geometry-driven effects
  • Important features may be low variance
  • Not sufficient for inference tasks

Research Question & Motivation

  • Can flow fields enable inference?
  • Not only describe flows → but explain systems
  • Detect:
    • defects
    • anomalies
    • pathologies
  • Enable fast, data-driven decisions
  • Bridge Physics and Machine Learning
