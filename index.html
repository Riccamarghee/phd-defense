From flow simulation to system inference

  • Flow fields are observable manifestations of geometry, boundary conditions, and physical mechanisms.
  • The flow field can therefore act as an informative signature of the system.
  • Latent properties may be inferred from how they shape the resulting flow.
  • CFD becomes a natural tool for this purpose as CFD data are inherently informative.

Can fluid dynamics be used to reason about the physical system, not only about the flow?

Learning from flow-field signatures

  • ML models can extract information embedded in high-fidelity CFD fields.
  • Engineering defects or performance-limiting features may be identified from flow responses.
  • Biomedical airflow simulations can encode clinically relevant anatomical conditions.
  • This shifts CFD from a numerical evaluation tool to a medium for actionable inference.

Thesis focus

Coupling physics-based modelling with ML to reveal latent characteristics of physical systems.
